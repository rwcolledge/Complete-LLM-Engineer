\chapter{A Short History of Computing}
\label{chap:history-of-computing}
\chapterintro{Computing evolved from counting tools to programmable machines and cloud AI infrastructure.}
\learningobjectives{\begin{itemize}\item Describe major computing milestones.\item Explain programmability.\item Connect computing history to AI systems.\end{itemize}}
\section{Counting and Representation}Early computing began with representing quantities using objects, marks, and symbols. The important idea is that information can be represented by something else.
\section{Mechanical Calculation}Mechanical calculators showed that arithmetic could be automated.
\section{Programmable Machines}\begin{definition}A program is a sequence of instructions that tells a computer what to do.\end{definition}
Programmability lets the same machine solve many problems.
\section{Electronic Computing}Electronic computers replaced many mechanical parts with circuits, increasing speed and reliability.
\section{Cloud AI}Modern AI systems often run across many connected machines. This is distributed computing.
\begin{definition}Distributed computing uses multiple connected computers to solve a problem together.\end{definition}
\section{Exercises}\begin{exercise}Explain why programmability is more powerful than a fixed-purpose calculator.\end{exercise}\begin{solution}A programmable computer changes behaviour by changing instructions rather than rebuilding hardware.\end{solution}
